Conception

Lesson Two: Voice

You've considered writing styles. Now it's time to find your voice. This is what makes you unique, setting you apart from other writers.

Point of View

If you're a fan of Douglas Adam's Hitchikers Guide to the Galaxy, you may remember the point-of-view gun that makes people see things your way. I would love to have one of those myself, but that's not what Point of View means to writers. In writing, point-of-the view frames how you go about telling a story.

1st Person POV

One of the characters narrates the story.

2nd Person

Structured around the "you" pronoun, this perspective can be surpisingly challenging to write in. Generally used for choose-your-own-adventure style stories, but can be used to draw the writer in and make them feel like they are a part of the story.

3rd Person

The author narrates the story using he/she. There are three types of 3rd person writing: 1) Third person omniscient - the narrator knows everything about the characters. 2) Third person limited omnisicent - The author sticks closely to one character but stays with 3rd person. This is helpful for controlling what the reader knows. 3) Third person objective - neutral narrator and observational tone.
